June 5, 2026
Introduces the use of wearable device data for the algorithmic detection of postural orthostatic tachycardia syndrome (POTS) in post-COVID-19 patients, covering the study design, core methodology, and key findings of a proof-of-concept study.

Lecture 7: Acute Effects of Physical Exercise on Biomarkers of the Trace Elements Selenium, Zinc, Copper, and Iron

May 22, 2026
Introduces the micronutrient dimension of exercise immunology, presenting original data on the acute effects of a standardised sit-to-stand test on selenium, zinc, copper, and iron biomarkers — demonstrating how exercise-induced haemodynamic changes, oxidative stress, and immune activation alter trace element distribution and highlighting the practical implications for sports medicine laboratory interpretation.

Lecture 6: Regular and Acute Physical Activity and Cancer Immunity

May 15, 2026
Extends the exercise–immunity framework to oncology - epidemiological evidence for the anti-cancer effects of physical activity, the role of myokines and exercise-mobilised immune effectors (NK cells, CD8⁺ T cells, γδ T cells), mechanisms of tumour immune infiltration, and the clinical potential of exercise-combined immunotherapy and adoptive cell transfer.

Lecture 5: Return to Sport After Infections

May 8, 2026
Applies the immunological knowledge from the preceding lectures to the clinical challenge of return to sport after infection, using SARS-CoV-2 as a detailed model. It covers clinical staging, return-to-sport algorithms, required medical examinations, and the emerging syndrome of post-COVID condition with its implications for exercise prescription.

Lecture 3: The Biphasic Immune Cell Response to Acute Dynamic Exercise and the Immune Cell Response to Regular Physical Activity
