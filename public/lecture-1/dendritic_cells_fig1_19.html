PRR activation drives migration through lymphatic vessels to regional lymph nodes. During transit the DC matures: phagocytosis ceases; antigen is processed and loaded onto MHC class I and II molecules.

Pattern Recognition Receptor (PRR) activation is the initial step in innate immunity.

Fig. 1.19 — Dendritic cells initiate adaptive immune responses. Immature DCs take up pathogens via macropinocytosis and receptor-mediated endocytosis. PRR activation drives migration through lymphatics to regional lymph nodes, where fully mature, non-phagocytic DCs present antigen–MHC complexes with co-stimulatory molecules, activating naive T cells and stimulating lymphocyte proliferation and differentiation.
