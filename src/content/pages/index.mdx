## Aims and scope

This lecture series addresses the bidirectional relationship between physical exercise and immune system function, integrating current evidence from exercise immunology, sports medicine, and micronutrient research. The overarching aim is to provide students and practitioners with a scientifically grounded, clinically applicable understanding of how the immune system responds to exercise stress, infection, and training load — and how these responses can be systematically evaluated and managed.

## Grading

Prerequisite for admission to the module examination:
- To achieve the learning objectives of this module, regular attendance at the module's seminars and exercises is required.
- Regular attendance is defined as missing no more than 15 percent of the class time in any given session.

Module examination (VSM II) in the form of three parts:
- Exercise Immunology (written exam, 30 min, pass/fail)
- Exercise Immunology (presentation/proposal, 50%)
- Applied Sports Medicine (presentation in the form of a practical teaching demonstration, 50%)
- Each module part must be passed
