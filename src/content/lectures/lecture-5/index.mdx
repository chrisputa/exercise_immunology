### 5.1 Exercise During Acute Infections

Particular responsibility lies with the attending physician regarding the decision about physical exercise during acute infections. Fever, chills, painfully swollen lymph nodes, pronounced general symptoms, identifiable infection foci, and symptom worsening after exertion require special attention. **Training may only be resumed after medical clearance!** (Puta et al. 2026).

### 5.2 SARS-CoV-2 Infection as a Model

SARS-CoV-2 initiates infection through binding of the spike protein to the ACE2 receptor. The early immune response involves activation of innate immune cells (neutrophils, monocytes, NK cells) within hours -- with NK cells being activated by type I interferons and IL-12 to contain virus replication in the early phase (Murphy and Weaver 2016). Simultaneously, dendritic cells take up viral antigens and migrate to lymph nodes to initiate the adaptive T cell response (Murphy and Weaver 2016). Effector responses peak between days 7 and 10 after symptom onset. A rapid CD4⁺ T cell response is associated with a mild disease course (Sette and Crotty 2021; Tan et al. 2021; Puta et al. 2026).

### 5.3 Clinical Spectrum and Return to Sport

| Course | Symptoms |
|---|---|
| Mild | Subfebrile temperature (< 38.5 °C), mild cough, sore throat, mild malaise, absence of dyspnea |
| Moderate | Pronounced malaise, dyspnea at rest, higher fever (> 38.5 °C), headache, muscle, joint, and limb pain |
| Severe | Hospitalization up to and including intensive care treatment |

### 5.4 Return-to-Sport Recommendations

Considering the phases of acute infection regulation, resumption of activities after **10 days of rest including 3 consecutive symptom-free days** appears to be a safe approach. For asymptomatic infection: at least 3 days of training rest after diagnosis. Competitions only after 10 symptom-free days (Steinacker et al. 2022; Haunhorst et al. 2023a; Puta et al. 2026).

### 5.5 Medical Examinations

For athletes wishing to return earlier than 10 days: history taking, physical examination, laboratory diagnostics (differential blood count, CRP, transaminases, CK, creatinine, troponin if applicable), resting ECG. Myocardial or pulmonary diseases may require annual examinations including ECG, echocardiography, and arterial blood gas analysis (Steinacker et al. 2022; Halle et al. 2021; Puta et al. 2026).

### 5.6 Post-COVID Condition

Persistent symptoms affect a low single-digit percentage of athletes. Pathophysiological bases include tissue hypoxia and hypoperfusion, morphological changes in red blood cells, microcoagulation, vasoactive autoantibodies, and sustained immune activation. Based on current evidence and experience with ME/CFS, **no progressive training programs** can be recommended for post-COVID condition (Altmann et al. 2023; Haunhorst et al. 2022, 2024; Zhang et al. 2025; Puta et al. 2026).
